# Getting Started with Axtra Landing Page Builder

## Quick Start Guide

### Prerequisites
- A modern web browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge)
- Basic understanding of your business and landing page goals
- Optional: Competitor references or inspiration sites

---

## Step-by-Step Tutorial

### 1. Open the Builder

1. Navigate to `src/index.html` in your web browser
2. You'll see the welcome screen with an introduction
3. Click **"Start Building ‚Üí"** to begin

---

### 2. Select Your Industry (Step 1)

**Why this matters:** Industry selection helps us suggest relevant competitors and best practices.

1. Click the "Select Your Industry" dropdown
2. Choose the industry that best matches your business
3. After selecting, you'll see competitor examples appear below
4. **Optional:** Check boxes next to competitors whose landing pages you admire (max 3)
5. **Optional:** Add a custom reference URL if you have another site in mind
6. Click **"Continue ‚Üí"**

**Tips:**
- Choose "Other / Custom" if your industry isn't listed
- Competitor selection is helpful but not required
- You can reference specific pages, not just homepages

---

### 3. Define Your Objective (Step 2)

**Why this matters:** The landing page objective determines which sections and components work best.

1. Review the 8 objective options displayed as cards
2. Click on the card that matches your primary goal:
   - **Lead Generation** - Collect emails/contact info
   - **Direct Sales** - Sell a product
   - **Webinar** - Register attendees
   - **Product Launch** - Announce new product
   - **Awareness** - Educate about brand
   - **Signup** - Free trial registration
   - **Download** - Offer content download
   - **Event** - Event registration
3. **Optional:** Enter how you'll measure success
4. Click **"Continue ‚Üí"**

**Example success metrics:**
- "100 signups in first month"
- "5% conversion rate"
- "500 downloads in launch week"

---

### 4. Choose Design Style (Step 3)

**Why this matters:** Design style sets the visual tone and aesthetic direction.

1. Browse through the design style options
2. Click on the style that fits your brand:
   - **Minimal & Clean** - Lots of whitespace, simple
   - **Bold & Impactful** - Strong visuals, vibrant
   - **Professional & Corporate** - Traditional, trustworthy
   - **Creative & Artistic** - Unconventional, expressive
   - **Elegant & Sophisticated** - Refined, luxurious
   - **Modern & Trendy** - Contemporary, current
   - **Playful & Fun** - Friendly, energetic
   - **Technical & Data-Driven** - Detailed, analytical
3. Select a color preference:
   - **Axtra system** (recommended) - Professional grayscale
   - **Brand colors** - Provide your own colors
   - **AI suggest** - Let AI choose based on industry
4. If you chose "Brand colors", pick 3 colors using the color pickers
5. Click **"Continue ‚Üí"**

**Tips:**
- Match the style to your target audience expectations
- "Minimal & Clean" works well for most B2B and SaaS
- "Bold & Impactful" great for e-commerce and consumer products

---

### 5. Enter Business Details (Step 4)

**Why this matters:** This information personalizes the landing page content.

1. **Business/Product Name** - Enter your company or product name
2. **Business Description** - Write 2-3 sentences about what you do
3. **Target Audience** - Describe your ideal customer
4. **Primary Language** - Select the content language
5. If you select "Other" for language, specify which one
6. Click **"Continue ‚Üí"**

**Example business description:**
> "We provide AI-powered marketing tools that help small businesses automate their social media posting and content creation. Our platform saves marketers 10+ hours per week."

**Example target audience:**
> "Small business owners and marketing managers at companies with 10-50 employees who want to streamline their marketing without hiring an agency."

**Tips:**
- Be specific in your descriptions
- Focus on benefits, not just features
- Think about your ideal customer, not everyone

---

### 6. Define Content & Messaging (Step 5)

**Why this matters:** This is the actual copy visitors will see.

1. **Main Headline** - Your primary value proposition (most important!)
2. **Sub-Headline** (optional) - Supporting text
3. **Value Propositions** - Add 3-6 key benefits
   - Click **"+ Add Value Proposition"** to add more
   - Click **"Remove"** to delete extras
4. **CTA Text** - What the button should say
5. **CTA Style** - Choose button appearance
6. **Secondary CTA** (optional) - Alternative action
7. Click **"Continue ‚Üí"**

**Example headlines:**
- "Build Landing Pages in Minutes, Not Hours"
- "Social Media Marketing That Runs on Autopilot"
- "The CRM That Actually Gets Used"

**Example CTAs:**
- "Get Started Free"
- "Book a Demo"
- "Download Guide"
- "Start Your Trial"

**Tips:**
- Make headlines benefit-focused, not feature-focused
- Keep headline under 10 words if possible
- Use action verbs in your CTA

---

### 7. Add Social Proof & Features (Step 6)

**Why this matters:** Social proof builds credibility and trust.

1. **Social Proof Types** - Check any that apply:
   - Customer Testimonials
   - Client/Partner Logos
   - Key Statistics
   - Reviews/Ratings
   - Case Studies
   - Press/Media Mentions
2. **Key Features** (optional) - Add main features to highlight
   - Click **"+ Add Feature"** to add more
3. **Pricing Section** - Choose:
   - Yes, show pricing
   - Show "Contact for Pricing"
   - No pricing section
4. Click **"Continue ‚Üí"**

**Tips:**
- More social proof = more trust
- If you're new, focus on other value indicators
- Choose pricing option based on your sales model

---

### 8. Set Technical Preferences (Step 7)

**Why this matters:** Defines functional requirements beyond design.

1. **Lead Form Fields** - Select which fields your form needs:
   - Email (always included)
   - Full Name
   - Company Name
   - Phone Number
   - Message/Comments
2. **Integrations** (optional) - List tools to integrate with
3. **Special Requirements** (optional) - Any other notes
4. Click **"Review & Generate ‚Üí"**

**Example integrations:**
- "Mailchimp for email list"
- "HubSpot CRM"
- "Google Analytics, Facebook Pixel"

**Tips:**
- Keep forms short for higher conversion
- Only ask for information you'll actually use
- Mention accessibility needs in special requirements

---

**Why this matters:** Last chance to ensure everything is correct.

1. Review the summary of all your inputs

## Troubleshooting

**"I can't proceed to the next step"**
- Check for error messages in red
- Ensure all required fields are filled
- Required fields have a red asterisk (*)

**"My competitor examples didn't appear"**
- Make sure you selected an industry first
- Some industries have limited competitor data
- You can always add a custom URL

**"The generated prompt seems incomplete"**
- Go back and check all steps were completed
- Ensure you filled in required fields
- Try regenerating after reviewing inputs

**"I want to start over"**
- Click "Start New" in the header on the result page
- Or refresh the browser on any step (will lose progress)
